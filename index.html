HISTORY

Same great taste for over 75 years

Old Bay Seasoning, a blend of herbs and spices, was created by Gustav Brunn, a German immigrant in 1939. Prior to immigrating to the US, Brunn started a wholesale spice and seasoning business in Wertheim, Germany. Following the rise of Nazi power, Brunn moved his company to Frankfurt, Germany. However, not shortly after he escaped Nazi concentraction camps during WWII and relocated to Baltimore, MD where he founded the Baltimore Spice Company. There he produced “Delicious Brand Shrimp and Crab Seasoning”, which today is called Old Bay. The seasoning is named after the Old Bay Line, a passenger ship line that ferried passengers from the Chesapeake Bay in Baltimore to Norfolk, Virginia. In 1990, McCormick & Co purchased the seasoning brand. To this day, it is still produced. With 8.3 million units sold each year, Old Bay is found worldwide. Old Bay is not an ordinary seasoning to most, it is used as a versatile blend to enhance almost anything including my personal favorite, crab dip.


FEATURED RECIPE

Old Bay Crab Dip

Embrace the flavors of Old Bay and its Maryland roots by making a simple but delicious dip. Incoporating a mixture of lump crab meat and Old Bay, this is sure to be a fan favorite appetizer.

Ingredients

  • 1 package (8 ounces) cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 teaspoons OLD BAY® Classic Seafood Seasoning
  • 1/2 teaspoon McCormick® Ground Mustard
  • 1 pound lump crabmeat
  • 1/4 cup shredded Cheddar cheese

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Mix cream cheese, mayonnaise, Old Bay Seasoning and ground mustard in medium bowl until well blended. Add crabmeat; toss gently.
  2. Spread in shallow 1 1/2-quart baking dish. Sprinkle with Cheddar cheese and additional Old Bay Seasoning, if desired.
  3. Bake 30 minutes or until hot and bubbly. Serve with assorted crackers or sliced French bread.
